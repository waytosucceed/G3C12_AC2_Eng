Verbs


Convention/Grammar

A verb is a word that expresses an action, occurrence, or state of being. Verbs are essential to sentences as they describe what the subject is doing or what state the subject is in.

An action verb is a verb that describes a physical or mental action performed by the subject. Action verbs express specific movements, activities, or processes happening.

They answer the question "What is the subject doing?" Some examples of action verbs are run, jump, think, bake, sing, and dance.



Words that I learnt from the story

Huffed: To puff out the air or breathe forcefully, often in a display of exertion or frustration.
Sentence: The wolf huffed and puffed, trying to blow down the brick house.

Steam: The hot vapour into which water is converted when heated to the boiling point
Sentence: The pot on the stove released a cloud of steam as the water inside began to boil.

Scared: Feeling fear or anxiety, often as a result of an actual or perceived threat or danger.
Sentence: The child was scared of the thunder and lightning during the storm.
